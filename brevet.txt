PATENT APPLICATION DRAFT - PRIOR ART DISCLOSURE
Title: Method and Apparatus for Continuous Production of Diamond Filament via Maser-Assisted Chemical Vapor Deposition

Inventors: [Ton pseudo GitHub], KSP Division, Kerbal Space Center
Date of Public Disclosure: 17 April 2026
License: Public Domain / CC0 - Prior Art pour empêcher brevet fermé

ABSTRACT
A method and apparatus for the continuous synthesis of diamond filaments, ribbons, or cables by means of localized micro-plasma generation using coherent microwave radiation (maser or gyrotron) directed at a moving substrate wire. The substrate traverses a reaction zone wherein carbon-containing precursor gases are decomposed by the plasma, depositing diamond phase carbon onto the substrate to form a continuous diamond-coated or monolithic diamond filament. The invention enables production of diamond structures of arbitrary length, overcoming the substrate size limitations of conventional MPCVD batch processes.

BACKGROUND OF THE INVENTION
**** Conventional diamond synthesis via Microwave Plasma Chemical Vapor Deposition (MPCVD) is limited to batch processing on planar substrates, typically silicon or silicon carbide wafers <200mm diameter. Maximum thickness growth rates ∼10 µm/h limit production to thin films or plates.[0001] 

**** Applications requiring long-length diamond structures, including space elevator tethers, high-strength cables, and continuous fibers, cannot be manufactured by batch MPCVD due to exponential defect accumulation and reactor size constraints.[0002]

**** There exists a need for a continuous, scalable process to produce diamond filaments of indefinite length with controlled diameter and tensile properties exceeding 100 GPa.[0003]

SUMMARY OF THE INVENTION
**** The present invention discloses a continuous-feed CVD system wherein a thin substrate wire of tungsten, molybdenum, or sacrificial carbon is drawn through a reaction zone.[0004]

**** One or more coherent microwave sources (masers, gyrotrons) operating at 1-300 GHz with total power 1-100 kW are focused to intersect at the wire, creating a localized plasma volume of 0.1-10 mm³ at 2000-5000K.[0005]

**** Process gases comprising H₂ 90-99.9% and CH₄ 0.1-5%, optionally with N₂ 0.1-2% or O₂ 0.1-1% additives, are directed at the plasma zone via gas shroud.[0006] 

**** Diamond phase carbon deposits epitaxially on the moving wire at 0.1-10 mm/s, producing continuous diamond filament. Wire take-up speed and maser power determine final diameter 1-1000 µm.[0007]

DETAILED DESCRIPTION
**** Figure 1: Substrate wire unspools from feed reel, passes through gas shroud, intersects focal point of masers, forms plasma, exits as diamond filament to take-up reel.[0008][10][12][14][16a][16b][16c][18][20][22]

**** Masers preferred embodiment: Gyrotron 95 GHz, 3 kW each, beam waist <0.5mm. Three beams at 120° for symmetric heating.[0009]

**** Gas shroud provides concentric flow: inner H₂/CH₄, outer Ar curtain to exclude atmospheric O₂/N₂, preventing plasma quench.[0010][14]
